From Proposal to Publication: Together

AESRN is a research network created specifically for emerging African health researchers who have the skills but need the collaborative environment to publish and advance their careers.

We bridge the gap between individual potential and published impact by creating teams, providing mentorship, and navigating the publication process together.

Our Unique Approach

AESRN is not an NGO or traditional research institute. We are a peer network of emerging health researchers across Africa who:

Collaborate on Publications

We match researchers with complementary skills to co-author papers that advance health sciences literature.

Learn Through Doing

Members gain practical experience in the entire research process—from proposal to publication.

Build Career Portfolios

Together we create publication records that support academic advancement and career opportunities.

The Publication Process Journey

1

Skill Matching

We identify complementary skills among members—epidemiologists, statisticians, clinicians, public health experts—and form balanced teams.

2

Proposal Development

Teams work together to develop research questions, methodologies, and proposals with guidance from experienced mentors.

3

Collaborative Research

Distributed teams conduct research across institutions and countries, leveraging diverse perspectives and local expertise.

4

Publication Preparation

We navigate manuscript preparation, journal selection, and the submission process as a collective learning experience.

Health Research Focus Areas

Our network concentrates on health sciences research where emerging African scholars can make significant contributions. We prioritize areas where collaborative approaches yield stronger publications.

Infectious Disease Epidemiology

Collaborative studies on malaria, TB, HIV/AIDS, and emerging pathogens with focus on local transmission patterns, interventions, and prevention strategies.

Non-Communicable Diseases

Research on cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, cancers, and mental health disorders in African populations, addressing unique risk factors and care pathways.

Maternal & Child Health

Studies on antenatal care, birth outcomes, childhood nutrition, vaccination coverage, and interventions to reduce maternal and child mortality.

Pharmacology & Drug Development

Research on medication adherence, adverse drug reactions, traditional medicine integration, and clinical trials tailored to African genetic diversity.

Genomics & Precision Medicine

Collaborative projects on genetic predispositions to diseases, pharmacogenomics, and developing precision medicine approaches for African populations.

Health Systems & Digital Health

Research on healthcare access, quality improvement, telemedicine applications, and digital tools for health service delivery in resource-limited settings.
